Ravi Kumar

Senior Research Fellow & PhD Candidate · Department of International Relations & Politics · Central University of Kerala

ORCID: 0000-0001-5928-6339 Course Work GPA: 8.80 (PhD) UGC-JRF (Political Science)

I am a Senior Research Fellow and PhD Candidate at the Central University of Kerala, working on the role of status in rising powers’ international relations with India as a case study. My research engages International Relations theory, status-seeking strategies, and regional order-building in the Indo-Pacific, including the SAGAR and MAHASAGAR initiatives. I also use quantitative and mixed methods for policy-relevant research, with tools such as R and Stata.

Working Papers

Under review / in preparation

1

India’s Status in the Indo-Pacific region: Identity management, QUAD, and the SAGAR

Submitted to The Round Table · Sept 2025 · Under Review

Applies SIT to India’s status-seeking via social mobility (QUAD), competition (SAGAR), and creativity (aid diplomacy).

2

India and the Global Nuclear Order: Seeking Status Through Identity Management Strategy

Submitted to The International Spectator · Dec 2025 · Under Review

Analyzes India’s NSG membership quest through phases of competition, creativity, and mobility; discusses “status denial” and institutional impermeability.

3

Is India Making a Maritime Security Multiplex?

With Dr Ladhu Ram Choudhary · Part of ICSSR project

Tracks evolution from SAGAR to MAHASAGAR using the concept of multiplexity; includes declassified records and an expert survey (n=73).

4

Multiplex, Multipolarity and India’s Status Seeking in World Order

Manuscript in preparation

Examines India’s strategic navigation between multipolarity and multiplex order and implications for legitimacy and global governance architecture.

5

Great Power Status and Re-engineering of the region: India’s status seeking in the Indo-Pacific through SAGAR and MAHASAGAR

Manuscript in preparation

Explores regions as both status enablers and constrainers, and how regional “re-engineering” shapes rising power status claims.
